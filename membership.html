FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

  • What is Live It Training?
    Live It Training transcends exercise. You will get stronger and more athletic. You will learn skills to meet yourself and life at a deeper level. Live It Training takes the best components of physical culture, exercise physiology, behavioral science and mindfulness. This program has helped thousands of people lead healthier, happier and richer lives.
  • Can beginners use Live It Training?
    Yes. Today is the best day to start. We will give you extra realtime guidance during your initial sessions to help get you going. Our certified Athletic Trainer will teach you the skills you need to begin. Wherever you are starting from, we will meet you there. Let’s do this.
  • What are Lean, Strong, and Loose classes?
    Live It Lean
    The next evolution of HIIT. Multi joint, compound exercises performed with intention and focus. Shed body fat and build cardiovascular capacity as you energize your body and mind.

    Live It Strong
    Functional, athletic strength with skill. Change your physique. Change how you move. Channel your inner athlete and wake up to the sense of your own power.

    Live It Loose
    Athletic mobility, yoga and regeneration. Move fluidly with enhanced core strength and flexibility. Finish each session grounded and ready to meet the moment.

  • What is recommended?
      We recommend that you show up for two or more sessions per week. Attend one Lean, one Strong and one Loose each week for best results.

    You will transform. Like so many others in the Live It Training community you will notice a clearer mind and fitter body come alive.
  • How much space do I need?
    Almost all of your work will be on your high quality yoga mat. You will want about 2’ to 3’ feet of clear space around your mat. You will need a ceiling that allows you to extend your arms above your head.
  • What tools do I need to get started?
    You need a computer or TV to connect to the live classes via Zoom. You will also need a high quality yoga mat and some weights. In your initial call, we can discuss some of the options that might work best for you based on your size, experience, and goals.
